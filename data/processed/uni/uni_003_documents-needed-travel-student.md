## Traveling Outside the U.S.

If you're traveling to a country other than your home -- including international cruises -- check the website of your destination's [embassy](https://www.embassy.org/embassies/) or [NY-based consulate](https://www.tripsavvy.com/foreign-consulate-offices-in-nyc-1613011) to learn about their visa requirements for visitors. If needed, request your enrollment certification through [Vergil](https://vergil.columbia.edu/).

CUIT (Technology Information) has compiled important considerations for the Columbia University community for [protecting data security](https://cuit.columbia.edu/data-security-guidelines-international-travel) when you travel.

The [GlobalTravel website](https://globaltravel.columbia.edu/content/international-travel-tips-non-immigrant-visa-holders) centralizes information and tools for all members of the Columbia community whose study, research, or teaching involves international travel.

Visit [Step 2](https://isso.columbia.edu/content/entering-us-f-j-students#/text-9359) of our Entering the U.S. page (for students) as a reminder of what to expect.

## Troubleshooting Potential Problems

## 1. Your U.S. visa has expired OR will expire before your return

### Generally, you need an unexpired U.S. visa to return to the U.S.

There are two exceptions:

- Canadians are exempt from the visa requirement (but do require an I-20/DS-2019).
- When you return return to the U.S. from
**Canada, Mexico, or an "Adjacent Island"**you may be eligible for "automatic revalidation". Visit[this webpage](https://isso.columbia.edu/content/returning-canada-mexico-and-adjacent-caribbean-islands)to learn about these special provisions.

U.S. visas cannot be obtained in the United States. You can apply for a new visa only at a U.S.consulate or embassy, so be mindful of visa application procedures and the time needed for visa processing when you make your travel plans.

## 2. You're already abroad and you don't have your I-20/DS-2019 with you OR your travel signature has expired or will expire before your return

### Request a new I-20/DS-2019

Your new form will have an updated travel signature on it. Follow the instructions on our [Travel Signatures webpage](https://isso.columbia.edu/content/travel-signatures-and-passport-renewal#/text-4328).

### Too late to receive your new I-20/DS-2019?

You may have a problem being allowed to board by your airline at departure if you don’t have your COE (I-20/DS-2019) with you. If you have your I-20/DS-2019 but the travel signature is expired, they may be more lenient about permitting you to fly.

## 3. You're arriving in the U.S. without all required documentation

### Prepare for "Secondary Inspection" at Port of Entry

If you arrive in the U.S. without your COE or with an expired travel signature, you will likely be subject to additional screening by CBP (Customs and Border Protection) to verify your valid student status.

We recommend you carry documentation that can demonstrate your full-time student status, such as a printed copy of your registration status from [SSOL](https://ssol.columbia.edu/).
